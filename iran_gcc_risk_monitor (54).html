Tier 1: Most Credible 70–90% 50–75% 5–15% IRGC command & official outlets: Sepah News (IRGC official outlet), IRGC Public Relations Office, IRGC Aerospace Force, IRGC Ground Forces Command, Supreme National Security Council (SNSC), Interim Leadership Council. Numbered IRGC Statements (e.g., Statement No. 50). Tasnim/Fars only when explicitly attributed to named IRGC commanders or official IRGC communiqués. Direct, attributable statements from the IRGC command structure or the Supreme Leader's Office are the most reliable indicators of genuine intent. Sepah News is the IRGC's own outlet — statements published there carry the same weight as direct IRGC communiqués. Numbered IRGC Statements with hard deadlines and evacuation orders (e.g., Statement No. 50 — university ultimatum; 18-company list) have the highest operational specificity. Reporting from Reuters, ISW and CTP confirms this channel often precedes kinetic activity. High GCC interception rates (~85–96%) limit material impact. Strong correlation with subsequent launch attempts (ISW/CTP). Recent examples: Statement No. 50 (29 Mar) preceded confirmed university threat escalation; 18-company list via Sepah News (31 Mar) — same channel as 16 Mar UAE energy warning that was followed by strikes same day. When attribution is explicit and corroborated, follow-on attacks have often occurred within hours to days.
Tier 2: Moderately Credible 40–65% 25–50% 3–10% Iranian MFA (FM Araghchi), IRNA, Iran Parliament Speaker Ghalibaf (X posts), Parliament National Security Committee, senior government officials. Tasnim/Fars citing unnamed IRGC/government sources without independent confirmation. Conditional target lists published via state media (e.g., Fars bridge list, UAE infrastructure list). Policy signalling and intent framing — less directly coupled to operational decision-making than Tier 1. Conditional threats (e.g., "if Kharg Island is seized," "in response to B1 Bridge") fall here — they signal Iran's declared escalation doctrine without constituting operational orders. Ghalibaf X posts have elevated influence but do not represent IRGC command authority. Inconsistent linkage to follow-on kinetic activity. Recent examples: Fars UAE infrastructure list (27 Mar) — conditional on Kharg action, no execution; Ghalibaf desalination warning (22 Mar) — no confirmed strike; Fars GCC bridge list (2 Apr) — post-B1 strike escalatory signalling. Attacks typically preceded by Tier 1 corroboration, not Tier 2 alone.
Tier 3: Least Credible 10–35% 5–25% 1–5% Press TV, Tasnim and Fars when editorialised, unattributed, or interpretive. Pro-Iran social media accounts, Telegram channels without institutional attribution, anonymous "military sources." Unverified secondhand claims. Outlets frequently amplify rhetoric and psychological signalling, often overstating capabilities or intent. Note: Tasnim and Fars are not inherently Tier 3 — their tier depends entirely on attribution. Unattributed, interpretive, or vague claims about IRGC intentions with no named source fall here. ISW and CTP assess standalone unattributed media reporting as low predictive value. Low correlation with actual attacks unless subsequently confirmed by Tier 1. Claims primarily function as information operations or deterrence amplification rather than actionable indicators. Recent example: Oracle data center claim (2 Apr) — UAE immediately denied as "fabricated and incorrect."
